The story

Belarus → France.
The path.

Born in Minsk. Trained under Marčiulionis in Vilnius. Pro at 15 with Lietuvos Rytas. Blocked my way through D-I in San Diego, made All-WCC at USF and tore my ACL twice in one game (then a third surgery to rebuild it from the other leg), spent a year rebuilding through Utah, Austin, and a Spurs Summer League rotation, played a full year with the San Diego Clippers, and now I'm playing pro in France while building.

2013 — 2014 · Vilnius, Lithuania

Marčiulionis Academy.

Left Belarus alone at 13 to train at the Šarūnas Marčiulionis Academy in Vilnius. Two years under one of the great Lithuanian guards — first European to start an NBA game. Steel-tempered hands, footwork drilled until I couldn't think about it anymore.

Age 13.5 First step out of Minsk

2017 — 2021 · University of San Diego

"Squirrel."

Day one at USD, Coach Lamont Smith nicknamed me "Squirrel" — I was moving "99,000 miles an hour." Four years on, I became the 5th all-time shot blocker in school history. Graduated with a marketing degree. The name stuck longer than any contract.

#25
2021 — 2022 · USF San Francisco

All-WCC. Then three surgeries.

Grad transfer to USF on a Master's track. Made All-WCC First Team. Helped USF reach the NCAA Tournament for the first time in 24 years. In the WCC quarterfinals — the game that clinched the bid — I tore my ACL.

Then I came back in. Same game. Watched our lead bleed from twelve to four. Stepped back on the floor on a torn ligament, dunked off the bad leg, got a block, got a steal. Ran the lead back to ten. Next play, on the move, the knee went the rest of the way.

Three surgeries followed. The first was the ACL reconstruction. During it, a nurse touched the harvested graft and contaminated it — infection set in. The second cleaned everything out. For the third they opened my healthy leg, took a hamstring tendon, and rebuilt the ACL with that. Twelve months on the table, off the floor, in rehab.

All-WCC First Team 13.5 PPG · 9.4 RPG · 3 surgeries
2022 — Summer 2023 · The year off

A year off the floor. Then Utah.

Spent the year rebuilding the knee, finished a Master of Science at USF, and ran ops at Hokali — sourced coaches across the US, organized summer camps, made the marketplace run. Closed the year by stepping back on an NBA floor with the Utah Jazz in Summer League.
