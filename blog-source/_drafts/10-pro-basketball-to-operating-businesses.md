People ask me how a guy who played in Élite 2 ended up talking about search algorithms.

It wasn’t a career change born from failure. It was a shift in focus.

I spent years in the paint. Nantes Basket Hermine. The grind of European pro basketball. You learn two things fast: preparation beats talent, and data tells the truth even when you’re tired.

Today, I help D2C founders fix their brand’s visibility in AI search engines.

The game changed. The fundamentals didn’t.

## What does a pro athlete know about business?

Basketball is a contact sport played at high speed with zero room for error. One bad read on defense costs you the possession. One bad keyword strategy costs you the customer.

In the pros, you don’t get to practice "maybe." You execute or you sit.

Business runs the same way. Most founders treat marketing like a lottery ticket. They post content and hope something sticks. That’s not a strategy. That’s gambling.

I learned to look at the court like a board of data points. Every pass, every shot, every defensive rotation had a metric behind it. If I couldn’t measure it, I couldn’t improve it.

Now, I look at your brand’s digital presence the same way. Is your site answering the questions AI models are prioritizing? Are you capturing the intent before the competitor does?

If you can’t define the metric, you’re flying blind.

## Why AI search instead of classic SEO?

Classic SEO is about ranking on page one. AI search is about being the source in the answer box.

They are different games.

In the NBA, you could shoot 40% from the field and still lose because you didn’t rebound. In traditional SEO, you can rank #1 for a keyword and get zero revenue if the intent doesn’t match the offer.

AI search changes the battlefield. It’s not just about keywords anymore. It’s about entity clarity. It’s about structure. It’s about proving to an algorithm that your brand is the definitive answer.

I didn’t switch to AI because it’s trendy. I switched because it’s harder. And hard problems require better preparation.

## What’s the connection between sports and growth?

The connection is discipline in the face of uncertainty.

In basketball, you don’t know if the ball will bounce right. You control your footwork. You control your stance. You control your follow-through.

In D2C growth, you don’t control whether a founder books a call. You control the clarity of your offer. You control the speed of your site. You control the accuracy of your AI citations.

I helped negotiate aspects of the first G-League CBA. I learned how to read contracts, understand value, and negotiate for fairness. That’s exactly what we do when structuring a growth strategy. We find the leverage points in the contract with the market.
