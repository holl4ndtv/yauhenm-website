## How do I run this probe myself?

You don’t need a developer. You need curiosity and a free account.

1. Open ChatGPT (free tier is fine).
2. Ask a specific buyer question. Use "best," "top," or "recommend."
3. Ask the same question in Perplexity.
4. Ask the same question in Google AI.
5. Compare the top three results.

Note which brands appear first. Note which brands never show up. Look for patterns. Are they all household names? Are they all selling on Amazon?

## Why do those specific brands win?

AI doesn’t have opinions. It has probabilities. It predicts the next word based on what it has seen before. If a brand is mentioned millions of times across high-authority sites, it becomes the default answer.

A D2C founder I worked with learned this the hard way. He thought his product reviews would save him. They didn’t. The review sites linking to him had low authority. The big competitors had links from major publications and massive domain strength.

The AI isn’t judging quality. It’s judging consensus. And the consensus is built by volume, not just virtue.

## Does the answer change by engine?

Yes. ChatGPT leans on its training data and recent browsing context. Perplexity is more transparent about sources. Google AI blends real-time search with generative answers.

The brands that win everywhere are the ones with broad authority. The brands that win only on one platform are the ones with niche relevance. If you want to win across all of them, you need broad authority first. Then niche dominance.

## FAQ

### What is an AI search probe?
A probe is a controlled query test across different AI models to see how they respond to specific questions. It reveals which brands the AI prioritizes and why.

### Why doesn’t ChatGPT mention my brand?
ChatGPT mentions brands it has seen frequently in its training data. If your brand lacks broad authority or consistent citations, the model won’t include you in its recommendations.

### How can I get my D2C brand into AI answers?
You need to build high-authority backlinks, create unique data-driven content, and ensure your entity is clearly defined across the web. This requires a systematic approach, not just random posting.
