I used to think SEO was about my domain authority. I spent years perfecting meta tags, site speed, and internal linking. It worked for Google. It did nothing for ChatGPT or Perplexity.

The pattern is brutal but clear. When an AI answers "best D2C eyewear brand," it rarely links to the brand’s homepage first. It links to a Reddit thread from 2023. A Trustpilot review. A YouTube unboxing. A "best of" listicle on a niche blog.

I saw this with a specialty eyewear brand I worked with recently. They had perfect technical SEO. Zero mentions in AI answers. Why? Because their digital footprint was invisible outside their own domain.

AI doesn’t trust you. It trusts consensus. And consensus lives elsewhere.

## Do backlinks still matter for AI search?

Sort of. But not the kind you’re buying.

Classic SEO backlinks are about authority transfer. AI citations are about **mention frequency and sentiment**.

If your brand appears in a high-signal third-party source, it gets cited. If it only lives on your site, it gets ignored. The AI assumes if no one else is talking about you, you’re not worth mentioning.

You need "digital PR." Not the old-school press release type. The modern, fragmented, review-heavy type.

## Which review sites does AI trust most?

Trustpilot and Google Business Profile are the heavy hitters.

AI models scan these for sentiment analysis. A 4.8-star rating with recent, detailed reviews is a strong signal of legitimacy. A 3.2-star rating with complaints about shipping kills your chances instantly.

Focus here first. It’s the lowest hanging fruit. Fix your response times. Ask happy customers for reviews. Delete or resolve negative ones publicly. This is not optional. It’s your new homepage.

## Does Reddit actually help D2C brands?

Yes. But only if you stop selling.

Reddit hates marketers. If you post "Buy my socks," you get banned. If you answer a question about sock materials with genuine expertise, you get upvoted and cited.

AI uses Reddit threads as primary sources for "real user experience." They value the raw, unpolished opinion over the polished blog post.

Be helpful. Be anonymous if needed. Let the link come naturally. Do not force it.

## How to structure your off-site priority

You can’t do everything. Here is the order I follow for D2C brands:

| :--- | :--- | :--- |

| 3 | Authentic YouTube presence | Video transcripts are heavily indexed by AI |
| 4 | Niche Reddit engagement | Raw user validation; high trust score |

## How long does this take?

This is not a quick fix.

It takes months to build review volume. It takes time to earn respect on Reddit. You won’t see results in a week.

But once you’re there, it compounds. AI citations become self-reinforcing. More mentions lead to more visibility. Which leads to more mentions.

Ignore this for six months, and you become invisible to the next generation of search.

## FAQs

### Do backlinks still matter for AI search?
Yes, but they must come from high-signal third-party sources like reviews and reputable blogs, not just any directory link. AI values mention frequency and sentiment over pure domain authority.

### Which review sites does AI trust most?
Trustpilot and Google Business Profile are the primary sources AI uses for sentiment analysis. A strong rating with recent, detailed reviews is critical for visibility in AI answers.

### Does Reddit help D2C brands grow in AI search?
Yes, if you provide genuine value without selling. AI cites raw user opinions from Reddit more frequently than polished marketing content because it signals real-world usage.
