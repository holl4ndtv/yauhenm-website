**To check if your Shopify brand shows up in ChatGPT, run a cold-browser test using four specific buyer-intent questions. Do not rely on organic search results. Ask "best [category] brands," "is [brand] legit," "who makes [product]," and "[brand] vs competitor." Record the response across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Studio. If your brand is missing, you are invisible to AI-driven demand.**

## What questions should I actually test?

Most founders ask generic queries like "is this good?" That tells you nothing about commercial intent. You need to simulate a buyer who is ready to spend money but needs validation.

I use four specific prompts. They cover the entire consideration funnel. Run these in a private/incognito window. No cookies. No login history. This removes your personal bias from the result.

1.  **"Best [category] brands for [specific use case]"**
    *   *Why:* Tests top-of-funnel visibility. Are you listed as a leader?
2.  **"Is [your brand name] legit?"**
    *   *Why:* Tests trust signals. AI often pulls from review aggregates or forums here.
3.  **"Who makes [product name]?"**
    *   *Why:* Tests product-level attribution. Does the AI know you own your IP?
4.  **"[your brand] vs [competitor]"**
    *   *Why:* Tests direct competition. Are you positioned correctly against rivals?

## How many times should I run this test?

One run is a snapshot. It is not data.

AI models are stochastic. They hallucinate. They drift. If you ask the same question twice, you might get two different answers. You need a sample size to see the pattern.

Run each of the four prompts **three times** per engine. Use a simple spreadsheet. Columns for: *Response*, *Mentioned? (Y/N)*, *Sentiment (Positive/Neutral/Negative)*, and *Source Type (Blog/Review/Forum)*.

If you see your brand in 2 out of 3 runs, that is a win. If you see it 0 out of 3 times, you have a visibility problem. Do not panic yet. Check the next section.

## Does the test differ by engine?

Yes. ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Studio do not look at the same data sources. They rank differently. Ignoring one leaves blind spots in your strategy.

| Engine | Data Source Focus | Best For Testing |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ChatGPT** | Web search + internal knowledge | Brand legitimacy & trust signals |
| **Perplexity** | Real-time citations & sources | Direct links to product pages |
| **Google AI** | Google Search index | Commercial intent & comparison |

I run the full four-question set across all three. A brand might be invisible in ChatGPT but rank #1 in Perplexity for that specific competitor query. You need the cross-engine view to know where your actual demand is coming from.

## What do I do if I am invisible?

If you are not appearing, it usually means two things. Either no one is talking about you in a way AI can read, or your site structure hides your content.

First, check your technical foundation. Is your Shopify store blocking crawlers? Do you have a sitemap submitted to Google? If the base engine cannot find you, the AI chatbots won't either.

Second, look at your external citations. AI models pull heavily from third-party reviews, industry blogs, and social proof. If your brand only exists on your own domain, you are a ghost to the wider web.

You need external validation. This means getting featured in niche publications, encouraging detailed customer reviews on public platforms, and creating content that answers the specific questions above. It is not about tricking the AI. It is about being the most obvious answer to a human problem.

Remember: cited ≠ clicks ≠ sales. Getting mentioned is step one. Convincing the buyer is step two. You can fix the first part. The second part depends on your offer and site experience.

## FAQ

### Can I use Chrome extensions to check this?
No. Extensions often inject cookies or alter the DOM. Use a true incognito window or a fresh browser profile. You need raw, unauthenticated access to see what a cold user sees.

### How long does this audit take?
About 10 minutes per engine. With three engines and four prompts (plus repetitions), plan for about an hour of focused work. The spreadsheet takes longer than the typing.

### Does AI search matter more than organic SEO?
They are linked but different. Organic SEO is about ranking on a list of links. AI search is about being the direct answer. You can rank #1 on Google and still be ignored by ChatGPT if your content lacks authoritative context.

### What if I see my brand but with bad sentiment?
That is a trust issue, not a visibility one. AI pulls from reviews. If the sentiment is negative, no amount of SEO will fix it. You need to address the underlying product or service issues causing that sentiment.
